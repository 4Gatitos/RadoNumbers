## Lemma D (reversal duality — proven in the project, restated)

**Statement.** Fix a>=1, N>=1 and an integer c. With sigma(x)=N+1-x and c^ := a(N+1)-c, a 2-coloring chi of [1,N] admits a monochromatic solution of x1+a*x2-x3=c in [1,N]^3 iff chi∘sigma admits one of x1+a*x2-x3=c^. **Proof.** This is Lemma conj-reflect of nota/sec_conj.tex, fully proven there: y_i:=N+1-x_i gives y1+a*y2-y3 = a(N+1)-(x1+a*x2-x3), so sigma maps solution triples of E(c) bijectively onto solution triples of E(c^), and chi'=chi∘sigma preserves monochromaticity. ## Lemma V2 (2-adic transfer along the band)

**Statement.** Let c=(lam-1)a+t with t in [1,a-1]. Then (i) v2(a)<=v2(c) iff v2(a)<=v2(t); (ii) if v2(a)<=v2(t) then v2(a)<=v2(a-t) and v2(a)<=v2(2a-t). Hence existence of Rad2(a;c) transfers to existence for both reflected constants a-t and 2a-t.

**Proof.** (i) If v2(t)>=v2(a) then both (lam-1)a and t have valuation >=v2(a), so v2(c)>=v2(a). If v2(t)<v2(a) then v2((lam-1)a)>=v2(a)>v2(t) forces v2(c)=v2(t)<v2(a). (ii) Write v=v2(a). If v2(t)>v: v2(a-t)=v and v2(2a-t)>=min(v+1,v2(t))>=v+1>v (both 2a and t have valuation >=v+1). If v2(t)=v: a=2^v A, t=2^v T with A,T odd, so a-t=2^v(A-T) with A-T even gives v2(a-t)>=v+1, and 2a-t=2^v(2A-T) with 2A-T odd gives v2(2a-t)=v. In all cases v2(a)<=v2(a-t) and v2(a)<=v2(2a-t). **Proof.** Let N=M0, t>=2 (t=1 is the next lemma). Write g:=gcd(a,2t), b:=|a-2t| (b>=1 by Lemma V2). The existence condition gives g|t: v2(g)=min(v2(a),v2(t)+1)=v2(a)<=v2(t) and the odd part of g divides gcd(odd(a),odd(t)). Three cases on (chi(1),chi(t+1)).

CASE A: chi(1)=1. Only two constraints are used: no 0-pair at distance t (owner 2, color 0) and no 1-pair at distance delta (owner 1, color 1). (A1) chi(x+a)=chi(x) for all 1<=x<=N-a: if chi(x)=0 then chi(x+t)=1 (t-pair) and then chi(x+t+delta)=chi(x+a)=0 (delta-pair); if chi(x)=1 then chi(x+delta)=0 and chi(x+delta+t)=chi(x+a)=1; all intermediates lie in (x,x+a) ⊆ [1,N]. (A2) Hence phi:Z_a→{0,1} with chi(y)=phi(y mod a) on all of [1,N] is well defined (consecutive representatives differ by a). (A3) phi is t-antiperiodic on Z_a: for any residue rho pick x≡rho, x in [1,a]; since N-t=ta+2t+2>=a and N-delta=(t-2)a+4t+2>=a (t>=2), both pairs {x,x+t},{x,x+delta} fit in [1,N]; chi(x)=0 forces phi(rho+t)=1, and chi(x)=1 forces phi(rho+delta)=phi(rho-t)=0, i.e. phi(rho+t)=1 iff phi(rho)=0 — so phi(rho+t)=1-phi(rho) for all rho. **Proof.** Direct from Lemma D at each N<=lam-1 (all owners of E(c) on [1,N] have k<=N<=lam-1, i.e. lie on the left arm, and reflection carries them onto the one-sided system of E(c*_N)), Lemma conj-2adic of sec_conj.tex (v2(a)<=v2(c) implies v2(a)<=v2(an-c) for every n — so admissibility transfers; when it fails for c*_N the parity coloring transfers back through the reflection), and the cited proven values.
